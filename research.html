Featured Paper

Imaging the LkCa 15 system in polarimetry and total intensity without self-subtraction artefacts

C. Swastik, Z. Wahhaj, M. Benisty, S. Arora, C. Ginski, B. B. Ren, R. G. van Holstein, R. de Rosa, R. K. Banyal, R. Tazaki  |  arXiv:2512.18439  |  Accepted in Astronomy & Astrophysics

We present self-subtraction-free Ks-band total- and polarized-intensity imaging of the LkCa 15 disk using near-simultaneous reference-star differential imaging (“star-hopping”). We combine SPHERE imaging with ALMA continuum modelling and use phase-function + polarization diagnostics to constrain dust: compact Mie spheres are disfavoured, while porous aggregates match the observed scattering behaviour better. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

  • Self-subtraction-free disk imaging beyond 0.1″ using star-hopping RDI.
  • Phase function and polarized fraction used to diagnose grain properties.
  • Improved disk models with porous aggregates in the surface layer (with compact mm grains for ALMA).
  • Companion limits placed at wide separations (no new planet detection).

Research Areas

High-contrast disk imaging

Using SPHERE total intensity + polarimetry to reveal disk substructures (rings, gaps, spirals) with minimal artefacts and robust calibration.

Dust scattering & phase functions

Extracting scattering phase functions and polarization fractions to constrain grain porosity, aggregate properties, and surface-layer composition.

Radiative transfer modelling

Building self-consistent disk models with RADMC-3D that match scattered light and ALMA thermal emission, and testing grain populations across layers.

Host-star chemistry & ages

Studying the chemical environment and age distribution of exoplanet host stars to understand how stellar evolution and metallicity influence planet populations.
