Research Interests

My research seeks to understand the formation and evolution of planetary systems by studying the chemical properties of their host stars, exploring stellar ages and compositions, and conducting high‑contrast imaging of protoplanetary disks. Using advanced observational facilities and data analysis techniques, I investigate how the metallicity and age of stars influence planet formation and contribute to the modeling of disk structures around young stars.

Publications

Host‑Star Metallicity of Directly Imaged Wide‑Orbit Planets

C. Swastik et al., Astronomical Journal, 161, 114 (2021). This work examines how metal‑rich environments influence the formation of giant planets discovered through direct imaging.

Galactic Chemical Evolution of Exoplanet Host‑Stars: Are High‑Mass Planetary Systems Young?

C. Swastik et al., Astronomical Journal, 164, 60 (2022). Analyzing stellar chemical evolution to explore whether massive exoplanet systems are indicative of younger ages.

Age Distribution of Exoplanet Host‑Stars: Chemical and Kinematic Age Proxies from GAIA DR3

C. Swastik et al., Astronomical Journal, 166, 91 (2023). Combining chemical abundances and stellar kinematics to estimate the ages of exoplanet host stars.

Age Analysis of Exoplanet Hosting Stars from Isochrone Models

C. Swastik et al., Astronomical Journal, 167, 270 (2024). Using isochrone fitting to derive ages for stars hosting exoplanets.

Other Works

Additional papers include studies on carbon abundances in stars of the LAMOST‑Kepler field, artefact‑free imaging of the LkCa 15 system, and comprehensive modeling of protoplanetary disks.
